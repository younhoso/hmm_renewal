HMM Inside

HMM Inside

Angie Belalij / HMM Great Britain

Sailing along with beautiful sunshine, happy customers, great food and joined by JY Lee (EHQ MD) and Ted Song (EHQ Marketing Director) - what more could you want for the launch of the new Asia Europe Express service from Southampton?

I was tasked with organising the event for customers to welcome in the first AEX vessel to Southampton. If you know me, you know I love a list, so this task was a dream come true for me. HMM GBR with the assistance of Georgia from EHQ CMPM (who also took the photos) organised the invitations and promotional material, whilst I took care of getting everyone to the right place at the right time.

We hired the Princess Caroline to cruise around Southampton whilst the Hyundai Forward was in and working. Guests were greeted onto the top deck of the vessel with pastries and breakfast rolls, fruit juices and - of course - Prosecco as we set sail.

Rob Freeman from DP World was kind enough to give a commentary as we sailed around the terminal and viewed the Hyundai Forward, explaining everything from crane capacity to berthing details.

After we had sailed past the vessel it was time to enjoy a lovely buffet lunch. The staff of the Princess Caroline had everything organised perfectly.

An HMM member of staff was present during lunch at each table with our 50+ customers, and we were very pleased that every customer was happy to mingle and have a chat.

As we prepared for lunch, HMM GBR MD Peter Livey gave a great speech in which he spoke about HMM’s “20/20 Vision” and plans for future growth. After thanking customers for their support, he reminded them that as HMM invests in supporting global trade and going the extra mile to support their business needs, we rely upon their valued support for capacity expansion and new services such as the AEX going forward.

I was thrilled to see the customers post event social media activity around the event. Many have posted photos and others mentioned in written posts how great the day was. A photo of myself and Dave Barrett (National Sales Manager) received 3,700 views on LinkedIn alone. This is all positive exposure for HMM.

Also following this, a customer who (& I quote) “didn’t realise HMM were still going” have booked their first containers in years. Whilst on board, customers were discussing business they would like to switch back to HMM and new targets. So all in all, it was a very worthwhile day.

From a UK point of view, we could not have been prouder on that day, and I nearly had a little tear in my eye. Ships two and three are already over allocation, and this new service is a resounding success.
Well done HMM GB!
