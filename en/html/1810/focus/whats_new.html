HMM Holds
Hyundai Far
East’s 2018
Annual
Meeting of
the Board of
Directors

Soon Yong Kim / HMM HQ

HMM hosted Hyundai Far East (HYFE)’s 2018 Annual Meeting of the Board of Directors from the 16th to 17th of August at HMM Headquarters in Seoul, Korea.

Hyundai Far East is a strategic joint venture in shipping and logistics established between Shandong Shipping Corporation (SDSC) and HMM where SDSC is a dry bulk shipping operator with world-renowned reputation, ranking 3rd as a tonnage provider in China and 1st in Shandong Province.

Since its establishment in 2009, HYFE has been successfully performing its Consecutive Voyage Charter with China’s 2nd largest crude steel producer, Hesteel Group, and furthermore, expanded its area of business to Panamax chartering, involving transportation of coal for Chinese power plants and as general agents. Hyundai Far East has truly been exemplary in corporate cooperation of shipping and logistics between China and Korea.

A bridge of shipping, logistics and friendship between China and Korea

On the afternoon of 16th August, Vice President Maozhong Li of Shandong Shipping Corporation paid a courtesy call to President Changkeun Yoo where they discussed a variety of means for the development of the joint venture company and various measures of mutual cooperation and exchange.

Mr. Li also gladly extended his invitation to President Yoo to visit SDSC whenever his schedule allows.

The Board of Directors Meeting was held on the 15th floor of the West Building where the Board of Directors were comprised of Maozhong Li from Shandong Shipping Corporation, Ning Ma and Suhan Kim from Hyundai Far East, and Byungsoo Kim from HMM.

They shared in-depth discussions regarding the approval of key agendas such as the 2017 Financial Statement and cooperative measures in preparation of changes in the future shipping environment such as the IMO’s 2020 Sulphur Cap and more.

After the meeting, the members gathered at the ‘Crystal Jade’, a Chinese restaurant in the centre of Seoul, to enjoy a time for casual conversation along with fine dining and Chinese Baijiu.

The next day, they had the chance to individually meet up with HMM’s Cape/Panamax Team, Supramax/Handysize Team, Tanker Team and Heavy Lift & OOG Team. SDSC and HMM were able to exchange ideas on various cooperative measures while learning about each other’s business situation and resources.

Vice President Li, Shandong Shipping Corporation, commented that, “There is room for mutual cooperation and many hopes for securing new dry bulk and tanker fleets including new projects in the near future.”

Suhan Kim, Hyundai Far East Co., Ltd., said,

“Since September 2015 when I was posted in Hyundai Far East, I believe this year’s Annual BoD Meeting has been one of the most meaningful events with our partner companies. I am deeply moved by the fact that the BoD Meeting was finally held at HMM Headquarters for the first time in five years and thankful to all of those supported in preparing for the event.

Hyundai Far East, as the first of its kind dry bulk joint venture between China and Korea, is noteworthy and of unique significance.

I trust keeping a cooperative relation with Shandong Shipping Corporation as a strategic partner will be of great advantage to HMM as SDSC is Shandong Provincial Government’s strategic enterprise invested by key state-owned enterprises from different fields of Shandong Province, meaning that this relationship will in turn be a potential stepping stone to enter industries involved with SDSC, not only in shipping, but in logistics, trade, investment and more.

Shandong Shipping Corporation understands the importance of the joint venture along with the need to continuously move forward.

Though we may be experiencing some hard times, all hands from Hyundai Far East will remain committed to the best efforts to become a good model to all joint venture companies.”

HMM intends to strengthen its current businesses in China and expand into new areas of business, while making further endeavors to reinforce strategic cooperation with Shandong Shipping Corporation, as well as continue to build up sales capacity in China via Hyundai Far East.
