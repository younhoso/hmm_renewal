Life Goes On

Australia- Enjoy the Sunshine of the South Pacific

Sunghong Song / HMM HQ

What would you consider when choosing your summer vacation? Fine dining? Exotic scenery? Or thrilling experiences? Australia has them all. My trip to Australia gave me so many unforgettable memories, and in this article, I’d like to share them with you as well as some tips for those of you who are interested in visiting.

1. If you’re in search of unusual experiences, visit Australia and participate in its diverse activities.

As you may know, Australia has the most beautiful seascape that attracts many divers and surfers. Beyond water sports, Australia offers thrilling experiences such as skydiving. If interested, you can easily find a skydiving program thru a travel agency located in big cities such as Sydney or Brisbane. For me, I flew 4,000 feet above the city of Goldcoast. Fortunately, the weather was so fine that I could see the whole city and ocean. If you look down at the emerald green ocean from the sky, you will never forget that moment.

2. Hike the primeval forest of Blue Mountain.

62 Miles away from the city of Sydney, lies the ancient forests of Blue Mountain. Many visitors come to Blue Mountain to see the famous rocks known as the ‘Three Sisters’. A trip to Blue Mountain begins at the ‘Echo Point lookout. You can see three big rocks among the wide panoramic views. The next step to enjoy Blue Mountain is to dive into the forest. You can explore the eucalyptus forest along the hiking trail or by the cable car ‘Sceniscender’. According to tour guides, one of the exports of Australia is fresh air from Blue Mountain. Whether it’s true or not, the fresh air of Blue Mountain keeps your footstep light so that you don’t grow exhausted while hiking in the forest.

3. The most beautiful harbour in the world, Sydney

Naples of Italy, Rio de janeiro of Brazil, and Sydney of Australia have been constantly cited as homes to the most beautiful harbours in the world. The Sydney harbour is graced by the Opera House, the landmark of Sydney, and on the opposite side, many thrill seekers climb the Harbour Bridge. Along the pier, the street is lined with many restaurants and cafes where you can enjoy the beautiful scenery of the modern city.

Sydney is also famous for its night skyline. You can look down at the whole city from the ‘Sydney Tower Eye’, but note that it isn’t possible to see the Opera House from this vantage point. Ten minutes away from the Sydney Tower, you can find a harbour so darling that it is indeed aptly named so. The night streets of the Darling harbour will complete your trip to Australia.
