Life Goes On

School Trip to Norway

Lena Schaefer / HMM Germany

Oslo – one of the coziest cities of Scandinavia –

was the final destination of an unforgettable class trip

During my apprenticeship at HMM Hamburg, Germany, I did not only work in the office. In Germany every trainee also goes to a trading school, which is part of the German dual education system. In the office, I learn the practical things like proceeding bookings or the handling of dangerous goods. But at school, we learn everything about the shipping industry, no matter if it is about containerized or general cargo like bulks.

At the end of May, my trading class was allowed to go on a class trip, which had been planned all by ourselves. We not only had to find an accommodation and book flights, we also had to plan group activities. We decided to go to Oslo, the capital city of Norway.

The whole trip had to be related to the shipping industry which was the reason why we did a lot of maritime and shipping related activities: We visited DNV GL and had a presentation on the safety of vessels. We also went to a P&I Club, where we learned a lot about their task area and why it is so important for ship owners to have insurances for their vessels. But we also had the chance to discover one of the most beautiful Scandinavian cities during the day, but also at night. Not only Oslo, but also the whole Kingdom of Norway – also called “Noreg” or “Norge” by locals – has one of the world's most impressive landscapes. The country is lined by fjords, rivers and mountains. During the winter, you can even see the polar lights whining in the sky.

Our trip started with a lot of trouble due to a flight cancellation. We first found out about it after arriving at the airport at 6:00 AM in the morning. Luckily, we were spontaneous! We took the train to Kiel – a town in Northern Germany – and took the ferry to Oslo. Sadly, we lost one day in Scandinavia.

But even though everything started with a lot of trouble, we had a great time: We sat on top of the opera house at night and went swimming in one of the plenty fjords. But the coolest thing we did was going to a bar fully made of ice. It was very cold, but was a wholly unique experience. We also tried some Norwegian food which definitely was an interesting taste experience!

On the way back to Germany, we took the ferry again: This time we were even allowed to go onto the vessel’s bridge and into the machinery room. It was very interesting to see how people work on board.

Before we finally got back to Hamburg, we stopped in Kiel again and visited the German Naval Yards: A ship yard where a lot of military vessels are being developed and built by engineers and yard workers.

I enjoyed the trip very much, that is why I will go to back to Norway again in the end of September!
