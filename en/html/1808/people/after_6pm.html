After 6PM

The World Cup with HMM Germany Colleagues

Seungbeom Pyo / HMM Germany

The World Cup arrived again, and HMM Germany was excited not only because of the festivities, but also the interesting meeting at the group stage with South Korea. The two teams met in the last group stage match. Germany had lost its first match against Mexico and won against Sweden very dramatically in the last minute. Meanwhile, South Korea had lost two matches against Mexico and Sweden, and thus was nearly on the brink of elimination.

Pointwise, Mexico had secured 6 points against Germany and South Korea with plus two goal differences, while Sweden and Germany had 3 points with a zero goal difference, putting critical importance on the last group matches for all teams – South Korea, Germany, Mexico and Sweden. If Mexico won or drew a draw against Sweden, and Germany won against South Korea, Mexico and Germany would be the two teams, who qualified for the group stage. If Mexico won or drew a draw against Sweden, and South Korea won against Germany with more than a two goal difference, Mexico and South Korea would be the two teams, who qualified for the group stage.

These interesting possibilities kept all of us at HMM Germany very nervous, and we organized a special viewing event for the match. Our Hamburg office invited all family members and partners of colleagues to watch the match, ending the work day early since the match started at 16:00. We also arranged snacks and finger foods with beverages for everyone.

The match started, and all German and Korean colleagues watched together while eating and drinking. Somehow Sweden started to lead in its match against Mexico, which meant Germany just needed to win against South Korea with a single goal. However, no team could pull ahead, and while Germany had several chances, it was South Korea who put in the first goal shocking not only all around the world, but all of us watching from the Hamburg office. Finally, the match ended at a two-to-zero win for South Korea, which was one of the most surprising moments of the World Cup, that the defending champion dropped out in the group stage.

At HMM Germany, we couldn’t believe that Germany placed at the bottom of the group (South Korea finished in 3rd place). This was the first time in World Cup history that Germany had not reached the round of 16, and all German colleagues in the office were woefully disappointed.

However, in spite of the loss, we all agreed it was still a precious time where we could bond together, as we were sure that getting through this kind of event would help us stay stronger within our own challenges as we at HMM Hamburg continue to strive and grow.
