HMM Success-
fully Carries
Out First
Cotton Ship-
ment from Rio
de Janeiro to
Asia

Tatiane Oliveira (HMM Santos)

Building on its successful partnership with Sepetiba Tecon, HMM successfully carried out the first ever cotton shipment from Rio de Janeiro to Asia.

Typically exported from the ports of Paranaguá, Paraná and Santos, São Paulo with the help of Sepetiba Tecon, in October 2018, Hyundai successfully carried out the first cotton shipment from Itaguaí, Rio de Janeiro.

Cotton is a key commodity for Brazil which expects to close 2018 with a record for exports of the commodity, consolidating its position as #2 in the global market. Never in the history of Brazil has there been as much cotton harvest as in the last crop: 2.1 million tons.

According to executive director of Abrapa, Márcio Portocarrero, Brazil will also be able to raise planting in the new cycle to 1.4 million hectares, with production growing to close to 2.3 million tons.

While domestic consumption is stable at about 700,000 tonnes, exports are expected to reach 1.2 million tonnes, a record that could yield the country's second-highest position on the international market, trailing only the United States

Mr. Portocarrero reported that the largest total export volume so far had occurred in 2011 with a volume of 1.03 million tonnes, and the largest shipment in a single month was in October of the following year at 188 thousand tonnes. He remarked, “The outflow of this crop will enable Brazil to not only beat the record, but also surpass Australia and India, becoming the second largest exporter behind only the United States.”

The harvest has already been collected and part of it will still be housed until June 2019 before being exported out of the country. At HMM, we are proud to be part of this history and carry the future!
