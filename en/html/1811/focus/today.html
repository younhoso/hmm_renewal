HMM Today

Special Containers
Move Avocados
All Over the World

A Cargo’s Untold Story ‒ Part 2

Editor

Avocados, also known as “The butter of the forest”, are gaining popularity as a healthy diet food. What kind of path do avocados, which taste better when we eat them with hamburgers, sandwiches, or salads, take as they make their way to our dining table? In this issue, I will introduce the story of the avocado, which is a well-known super food popular all around the world.

Avocados Enjoy Boom as Awareness of Healthy Food Spreads

Avocados, known as 'the butter of the forest', have earned the fastest rate of increase for imported fruits as their benefits become known.

According to the distribution industry, Korea avocado imports have increased by about 13 times in seven years, and a so-called avocado boom is taking place.

As one of the nutrient-rich fruits growing in the tropical climate, the avocado contains 11 kinds of vitamins and 14 kinds of minerals, including lutein, fiber, and more, making it the Guinness Book of World's most nutritious fruit. In particular, it contains a lot of unsaturated fatty acids called good fat, thus is attracting attention as a diet and health food. Imports of avocados are explosively increasing in Asia, not only in Korea but also in China. Avocado import volume increased 160 times for the first time in four years last year.

How fresh avocados are delivered to consumers?

Avocados can take a month or longer to be delivered from the harvest site to the retailers, depending on the region. Therefore, the storage environment during transportation becomes more important. Avocados should have different storage temperatures set depending on the percentage of fat in them at the time of harvest. For example, if the fat content of an avocado is about 14%, the storage temperature should be maintained at about 7.5 °C to 7.2 °C for a certain period after harvest. Its best condition will be maintained only when the temperature is adjusted, such as during transportation (5.5 °C) and as the fat content increases (lowered to 3.5 °C).

Avocados also require special conditions to ensure that the packaged fruit do not experience temperature changes during sea transport. A special loading type is individually prescribed for the size of the inside of a container such that ventilation and cooling are evenly maintained.

Container transportation technology to create the best commodity value

What technology is utilized to maintain the best avocado condition in a sea transport process that takes more than a month? Avocados start to mature while producing oxygen (O2) and releasing carbon dioxide (CO2) and water (H2O) after harvest.

Rapid removal of oxygen during transport can slow down its maturing process and prolong its life. CA container (Controlled Atmosphere Container) plays a role in creating such optimal transport conditions. CA container is a container equipped with a “CA (air conditioning storage) equipment”, which regulates the amount of oxygen, carbon dioxide, nitrogen, etc. in the air to control the aging of fruits and vegetables and maintain the taste and freshness at the time of harvest. In particular, in a CA container loaded with avocados, the temperature is lowered by increasing the concentration of nitrogen (N) inside to 90% or higher and the concentration of oxygen and carbon dioxide is adjusted to a rate of 5%, respectively, to maintain freshness for a long time.

Placing orders of new containers to meet fresh cargo demand

HMM is also stepping up its efforts to respond to the surging demand for fresh cargo. HMM is currently spurring the attraction of high value-added cargo, including placing orders of 4,500 ripper containers of the 4HRF (HC Reefer Container) specifications recently. Of the newly ordered ripper containers, there are 160 CA containers.

HMM has also recently expanded its service, which was limited to the US western coast route, to the South American route, including Mexico, Peru, and Chile, increasing profitability in the frozen/refrigerated cargo market.

In addition to this, additional orders of new CA container equipment are scheduled in the future. Thus, HMM will continue to strengthen its expertise in the avocado market from the USA’s Pacific coast and South America to the Far East. Avocados, which have made our diet healthy with “one meal” and sometimes “one glass”, have become a part of our daily lives as it travels in a container.

Logistics that have changed the tastes of people around the world due to fruit shipping technology and efforts are also being made to speculate what kind of change will take place.

What is a CA container?

With its temperature able to be set from minus 30 degrees to 30 degrees, the CA container is a special reefer container that can control the concentration of internal gas. This container can transport mature fruit fresh until they reach their destinations by adjusting the gas concentration, such as oxygen/carbon dioxide. Common cargoes include avocados and blueberries.
