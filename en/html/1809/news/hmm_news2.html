HMM’s Award for Excellent Fuel Saving Vessels

HMM selected six “Excellent Container Fuel Saving Vessels” in the second quarter and awarded prizes.

The “Award for Container Fuel Saving Vessels” is a system, whereby HMM selects and awards the best fuel saving vessels by comprehensively evaluating fuel reduction amounts among vessels operating in the second quarter of 2018 compared to those of the first quarter and evaluating fuel saving amount compared to the second quarter average of fuel consumptions by vessels operating on the same sailing route.

Of the 71 vessels operating in the second quarter, a total of 40 vessels were evaluated, excluding short-term chartered ones. Also, 32 vessels saved fuel oil in the second quarter compared with the first quarter of this year, reducing usage by a total of 38,000 tons in the first half of 2018.

Among them, HMM selected the top six vessels including “M/V Hyundai Singapore” which occupied the first place by reducing 250 tons in average per voyage, and presented them with awards and rewards.

HMM has continued its efforts to reduce fuel consumption, and has reduced costs by actively participating in ship operation, including setting the optimal sailing route, adhering to the economic speed, and efficient operation and maintenance of the engines as well as installing basic equipment necessary for fuel savings.

The price of Bunker C oil (based on prices in Korea), which was USD 180 per ton in the 1st quarter of 2016, has risen to USD 475 per ton at present, which is a 170% increase in 30 months. This factor pressured shipping companies to increase freight.

HMM official said, "This kills three birds with one stone: reducing greenhouse gas emissions and improving profitability as well as fuel savings. This award will help improve the energy efficiency improvement activities by crew."
