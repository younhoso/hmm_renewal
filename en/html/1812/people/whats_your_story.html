Jino An (HMM HQ)

Breakbulk Conference America 2018 was held for three days from October 2 to 4 in Houston, Texas. HMM participated in this event for the second year in a row and promoted HMM’s major service changes, visiting major shippers and customers.

HMM promoted its special cargo services by participating in the Breakbulk Conference Americas 2018 (BBCAM) from October 2 to 3 with the participation of staff members from headquarters, as well as expatriates and local staff. In particular, in this year's event, HMM expanded its sales activities focusing mainly on project forwarders based on AHQ's mid- to long- term company- wide flat rack (FR) sales activation plan and strategy, and achieved noteworthy results, including receiving an order for the Intel projects from the Americas to China in 2019.

01Group Photo At BBCAM Event

Breakbulk Conference: One of the world's most prestigious conferences

The Breakbulk Conference (BBC) is one of the most prestigious conferences in the world, involving shipowners, forwarders, stevedoring companies, shipping companies and port authorities involved in heavylift cargo transport. This event has been held in five cities around the world, including the United States, China, the Middle East, Southeast Asia, as well as the largest Breakbulk Conference Europe ever held. In the BBCAM held this time in the United States, 326 exhibition booths were opened and a total of 4,751 people from 1,507 companies from 69 countries participated. Five container carriers - MSK, MSC, CMA, HPL and COSCO - opened their container booths to promote OOG and breakbulk using FR containers.

The event, which was held in Houston, Texas, USA, was comprised mainly of exhibitions of oil and gas companies relevant to the characteristics of the Texas region. The participation rate of the related industries showed a surprising figure close to 80%: the number of participants related to EPC (Engineering/Procurement/Construction) was 27% of the total participants, while oil and gas made up 24% of those attending, and manufacturing companies, 26%.

02Maersk booth view

03MSC booth view

In addition to exhibitions, the BBC also held presentations on market trends through major shippers and speakers, as well as conferences that convey know-how and new technology for heavylift cargo handling. A total of nine sessions were held featuring 36 speakers. The session where a six-year-old child participated as a speaker was the most impressive. The audience applauded this child's lecture which used a toy model made of materials such as LEGO to provide innovative solutions for heavylift cargo transport.

HMM’s unique breakbulk integrated transportation service

HMM’s Heavylift & OOG Team offers an integrated transportation service that combines and operates heavylift, FR, OOG and breakbulk together in the container ship business. In particular, HMM’s service that utilizes FR containers and breakbulk for project transport at the same time, especially by utilizing multipurpose ships (MPP/heavylift) and container carriers, has become a unique integrated transportation solution around the world. Consumer satisfaction in HMM’s integrated services is at the highest level, including the Baraka Nuclear Power Plant Project, Abu Dhabi in the Middle East, which was carried out based on these services, and the Duqm oil refinery project, Oman which HMM plans to carry out in the future.

04, 05Having a conversation about HMM’s service promotion.

HMM employees who participated in the event actively promoted these developments to shippers from around the world whom they met at the BBC. In particular, this conference, where shippers and major customers from across the industry gather, not only strengthens relationships with regional shippers, but also becomes an important place to find new business models. In 2015, we set up a booth at the same conference held in China at a time when HMM was experiencing difficulties. However, by attending the conference, we were able to promote our company and its drive, and improve our image.

06(From left to the right) Youngrok Yoon (HMM LA) and Jino Ahn (HMM HQ)

07(From left to the right) Brandon Mcguilian (HMM Houston) and Ramy Gobran (HMM LA)

In the future, the Heavylift & OOG Team will continue to participate in related conferences as a means of strengthening our network with shippers and expanding sales activities depending on the importance of projects and cargo in major regions. In addition, we plan to expand our sales activities by participating in the same conference held in China in March of next year.

I would like to thank those who conducted the promotion in detail for the BBCAM event at AHQ, including Executive Director Mr. Kim Jong-beom Yoon, Deputy Department Manager Mr. Yoon Young-rok of the LA branch, Mr. Ramy Gobran and Mr. John Szlosek of the NVO Desk, and Mr. Brandon McGuillian of the Houston branch.

  • * What is OOG (Out of Gauge)?
    OOG refers to cargo that can be shipped in a container carrier by loading it into a flat rack without a ceiling and side or an open top (which is a ceiling-less container) because the cargo exceeds a general standard container size.
  • * What is Breakbulk?
    Breakbulk refers to loading cargo that is too large in size or weight so that it cannot be stuffed in a container or packed, thus loading the cargo directly in a space in cargo holds or on the deck. In the case of a container carrier, a flat rack container is usually used when loading these types of cargo.
