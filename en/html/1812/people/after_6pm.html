HMM Mumbai
Family Picnic at
Della Adventure
in India

HMM Mumbai
Family Picnic
at Della
Adventure in
India

Viral Shah / HMM Mumbai

On 29 September 2018, the HMMI Mumbai (IHQ) office organized a family picnic to Della Adventure and Resorts situated near Lonavala in the state of Maharashtra.

For its park, Della Adventure is India's largest adventure park and corporate training facility offering an array of over 86 adventure sports and activities. Through the family picnic, HMM provided the opportunity for colleagues to invite and introduce their family members to other colleagues – making them feel like they were also part of the HMM Family.

Around 10 am, 130 participants reached Della Adventure and gathered at a decided spot to receive brief instructions given by a Park representative. After entering the park, the first thing we did was click the group picture below for lasting memories.

We all received the Day Adventure Pass, which gave access to 90% of the rides. There was something for everyone, including:

  • High Rope Challenge
  • Rappelling
  • Flying Fox
  • Land Zorbing
  • Bubble Soccer
  • Mountain Cycling
  • Horse Riding
  • Dog Walk
  • Artificial Rock Climbing
  • Archery
  • Shooting
  • Sports Bar

To remember our childhood days, a few of us started with double cycling, while others began with Horse Riding and the High Rope Challenge.

The High Rope Challenge activity was one of the most difficult of all, as it consists of different types of adventure stages which need to be completed. Rappelling was also a thrilling activity which tested our mountaineering skills. While performing the activity, we came to know it’s all about balancing and gripping.

Most of us also tried Archery and Shooting, but only a few succeeded in hitting the center target. The Flying Fox activity was also an awesome experience, where we could view the full park. Finally, the Sports Bar housed indoor activities like darts, virtual games, and traditional sports like carom and chess in a family-friendly atmosphere.

By evening, it started drizzling which was very pleasant. Our hearts were filled with delight, and little children were dancing in the rain. They were refreshed for the next day where we engaged in similar activities, and also enjoyed a delicious Indian lunch followed by evening snacks and high tea. At around 6.30 pm in the evening, we left from the park to return home.

We are thankful to our management for providing the opportunity to participate in such a great time of bonding, and look forward to doing again soon!
