HMM Carries Out Reefer Container
Performance Tests:

Achieving
Cost-savings with
Optimum Power
Consumption

Achieving
Cost-savings
with Optimum
Power
Consumption

Editor

In the era of high oil prices, the keywords of high efficiency, low fuel consumption and cost reduction have emerged as a hot topic in the shipping industry. HMM has begun responding to the high oil price environment by carrying out performance tests to introduce energy-efficient reefer containers.

HMM conducts tests for the introduction of optimal equipment

HMM conducted performance tests of reefer containers from November 5-9. In the testing held at Busan New Port, over 30 personnel participated, including HMM employees and engineers from four reefer container manufacturing companies: Carrier, Daikin, Star Cool and Thermoking.

HMM announced its management strategy of securing a "1 million TEU fleet" and achieving "sales of $10 billion" by 2022 at its Vision Declaration Ceremony held in October. Accordingly, HMM plans to order a large volume of container equipment next year, and will review the power consumption, price, performance, and other factors comprehensively based on the results obtained through these tests and to order the best reefer containers.

01, 02Participants gathered at the Busan New Port Auditorium presenting their products for each reefer container manufacturer.

03, 04Engineers checking if there is anything wrong with the equipment before conducting full-scale tests.

On-site efforts for fair testing

Prior to full-scale testing, personnel from the reefer container manufacturing companies and those from HMM gathered at the Busan New Port Auditorium and engaged in intense discussions about the test standards and items, and had a time for Q & A. The main test items were dehumidification performance by temperature, performance to reach 0 °C/-25 °C from ambient temperature, maintenance performance of a refrigerated/frozen cargo condition, and so forth.

HMM focused on ensuring the equipment of each manufacturer was tested in the same environment because the results of reefer container performance may vary greatly depending on various conditions. In particular, close attention was paid to the arrangement and power supply of the equipment so that the testing of each reefer container could be done fairly and safely.

Soon after, engineers from the reefer container companies moved to the container yard and started the full-scale tests. While large 50-meter-tall cranes were loading and unloading container boxes, the engineers were busy setting and checking their containers to make sure that cargo-loaded conditions for an internal test of each reefer container were the same and that a hygrometer (a temperature and humidity meter) was correctly installed.

05Engineers setting the power meter to check power consumption.

06, 07Engineers checking whether the cargo loading conditions
for a test of the inside of a container and a hygrometer have been correctly installed.

Expected cost-saving effects from high-efficiency reefer containers

HMM expects to reduce the fuel oil of the company’s ships and the diesel oil of the onshore power generators by placing orders for energy-efficient reefer containers based on the results of this test. HMM plans to furnish reefer containers with the latest specifications mainly to newly-built container carriers to be delivered in 2020.

Park Jong-il, Manager of the Container EQ Team at HMM, said, “This test is aimed at cutting costs by selecting a reefer container brand that features the best power consumption in response to the high oil price era. We are planning to order more than 10% of the current-level container equipment and more than 5% of the reefer containers in preparation for the fleet expansion to one million TEU by 2020.”

On the other hand, HMM continues to make efforts to save fuel as part of its energy efficiency improvement activities. As well as installing basic equipment necessary for fuel saving, it also saves costs by encouraging company ships to participate actively in these efforts, including setting the optimal route and observing the economic speed, in addition to efficient operation and maintenance of the engine.

08, 09Participants talking with each other while checking out the intermediate results of the test.

As a result, HMM comprehensively evaluated the amount of fuel reduction in the second quarter
of this year compared to that in the first quarter. It also evaluated the reduction amount compared to the average reduction amount in the second quarter on the same route, and selected and awarded ships with superior fuel reduction results.
