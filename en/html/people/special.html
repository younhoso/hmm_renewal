Travel:

Fulfilling Curiosity

James Hooper (Geographer)
james@james-hooper.com

My first memories of travel are layered with anticipation and excitement. A barrage of sights, smells and sounds, the vivid experience of visiting a new place. The infinite curiosity. Travel opens our eyes to our planet’s diverse places and introduces us to new experiences. In essence, I believe that travel provides unmatched learning opportunities, and an education that is wholesome, inspiring and enjoyable. So what can we learn when travel is our teacher and the world becomes our classroom?

Geography

Often the first thing you notice when you arrive in a new place is the difference in temperature, the sensation of swimming through a hot, humid wall of air in the tropics, or the sharp loss of breath when an icy gust of wind stabs your lungs in the arctic. We instantly recognise a different climate, and wonder how far we have travelled, what season it is and what latitude we are at on our planet’s surface. When we wonder why time or position causes temperature to change we must understand our planet is a sphere orbiting the Sun. Deserts and rainforests actively show us about the importance of water to life. Great mountain ranges attest to the powerful forces that that move the earth’s crust and how over epic timescales water and ice can erode the highest Rocky Mountains.

Physics

Even the process of travel cannot be ignored. Being thrown back in to your airplane seat as the engines reach full thrust teaches you about force and inertia, while looking out the window and across the wings makes you wonder how tonnes of steel does not fall from the sky. Boats demonstrate the nature of buoyancy and displacement, while great swells teach us about the behaviour of waves that is ubiquitous in water, light and sound. Skyscrapers are marvels to engineering and defy gravity as they shoot skyward, they make us curious about the materials, design and construction that can create cities in the sky.

History

A visitor to the middle east will not forget the call to prayer emanating from the minarets, while most European cities hold gigantic gothic cathedrals at their heart. Istanbul in Turkey has buildings that have changed between Christian and Islamic use over hundreds of years and bare this history on their stony skins. What civilisations built these huge monuments without modern machines? How could civilizations powerful enough to build the colloseums of Rome, the pyramids of Giza, and the temples of Teotihuacan cease to exist? What influence has this history had on the inhabitants of today’s souks and plazas, and how has it shaped the culture and religions we see today. The stark differences in the values and traditions around which societies base their lives can be shocking at first, but if you have experienced the human hospitality of strangers, and shared in their lived experiences, prejudices fade away to be replaced by empathy and understanding.

Language

Travel shows us the importance of good communication. Simple bad pronunciation has left me on the wrong side of cities, while even acquiring everyday necessities like water and food can become an epic challenge. Learning a few simple words can come with huge rewards as residents feel your respect for them and recognise your effort. It is hard to truly experience a place without sharing in the local language, and a desire to partake in the full range of cultural activities and friendships can be a huge motivation for learning a skill that is valuable both socially and in business. Noticing the similarities in separate languages can be fun and rewarding and even lead to a curiosity in the roots and nature of language itself.

Gastronomy

Eating new foods must rank among the most anticipated activities of any traveller. Different regions have access to unique plants and ingredients and distinctive flavours. Many countries have a proud range of national dishes lovingly produced by chefs and artisans who have spent decades building on and perfecting the recipes of their ancestors. There can be a thrill in trying a quirky dish, whether it be Frog’s legs in France, Guinea Pig in South America, or Kangaroo in Australia. Food culture is often at the heart of a society and seeing how locals share and enjoy it, and the rules and customs that govern when, how and with who you can eat provide and insight in to life in a country. The best food experiences stay with us for ever and can inform our own eating habits even after we return home.

Survival

Travel, whether for a relaxing beach holiday, or an ambitious challenge, is always an adventure. You can never know exactly what you will find in a place you have not been before, how you will react to the food and the environment, or how you will get along with the people you will meet. Entering the unknown is part of the beauty of travel. We are forced to adapt, to survive, and to find the things that we enjoy. We must draw on our most basic skills as humans; the emotional intelligence to deal with new situations and unfamiliar locations, and the navigational skills to travel around and return safely to our accommodation. We all have stories from holidays that went wrong and we were forced to draw on our resilience and ingenuity to get through. While at the time this can be upsetting, these stories often recount our proudest and most memorable moments – precisely because we struggled and we survived.

Next time you travel, try to remember the sensation you had the first time you journeyed away from home. Eagerly take in all of the little details and think about what they mean and represent. Go away hungry to learn and come home full of new experiences and knowledge. There is no better way to learn than through travel, appreciating all the amazing resources in our global classroom.

About James Hooper(Geographer)

James Hooper is a British mountain climber and adventurer who became the youngest Britons to summit Mt. Everest at the age of 19 along with his friend Rob Gauntlett in 2006.
James attained his undergraduate degree in Korea and appeared in a variety of Korean TV programs such as “Non-Summit”(비정상회담)
